Bank of British Columbia

Two Canadian banks have been named the Bank of British Columbia.





== The first bank: 1862–1901 ==

The first one was established by Royal Charter in 1862, with its head office in London. Between 1862 and 1871, it issued dollar banknotes. By 1885, branches operated in San Francisco, Portland (est. 1866), Victoria (est. 1862) and New Westminster (est. 1862). In 1889, a branch was established in Seattle.
