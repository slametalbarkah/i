Timeline of labour issues and events in Canada

This is a timeline of labour issues and events in Canada.





== 1700s ==

1799 – After establishing fur trading post Greenwich House at Lac la Biche, workers refuse to proceed to Lesser Slave River because of lack of provisions. First known strike action in Alberta.





== Early-mid 1800s ==

1803 – Seven men working for Peter Fidler at Lake Athabasca refuse to stay on job unless wages increased.

ca. 1812 – Dock workers in St. John (NB) and Halifax organize a union.

1842 – In Quebec, T.M. Moore begins to publish People's Magazine and Workingman's Guardian, the first labour-oriented reform newspaper.





== 1870s ==

1871 – Toronto Trades Assembly is formed. First central union body in Canada.
